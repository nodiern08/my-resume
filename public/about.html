About Me

Here's a little something about me.

My name is Nodier (and that's pronounced "No-dee-A"). I was born
and raised in Miami, FL's hot, sunny city. I am primarily Hispanic, as
my parents are Cuban, but my father's family comes of French descent.
So you can probably guess where the name comes from.

I grew up like most kids in the 90s; go outside and play with their
friends, then go back inside and play some video games and draw
pictures when I felt inspired. But I wasn't a techy kid until I reached my
early teen years when I had my first computer and started playing
computer games. Mainly MMOs like Runescape and World of Warcraft.
The good ol' days!

I'd explore the computer and try to understand the software. Click on
things and see where it would take me. I once opened my dad's
computer out of curiosity. I took it apart piece by piece, remembering
the order I removed its parts, then reassembled the computer back together
and felt like a total badass.

I did have an artistic side; I loved to draw and create characters inspired
by my favorite anime and video game characters. It was all just a glorified hobby.
But as I got older and worked at some terrible jobs, I later realized that my career
was unfulfilling, and I felt something was missing. It took many years to realize
I needed to pursue a career that wasn't just fulfilling but more "me."

So I changed career paths and pursued an artistic career. I studied traditional
and digital art, then learned to program, starting with Python, and earned some
certifications, including the PCEP.

Programming is an art within itself. There's something beautiful about code.
Watching your program come together is like seeing a painting come to life
as the picture develops after every stroke.

A website is like a canvas, and your code is the paint. Web Development is Art.
