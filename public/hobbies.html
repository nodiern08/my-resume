My Hobbies

I'll get straight to the point. My hobbies consist of 3 things;

  1. Gaming

    2. I grew up playing video games my whole life. The first video game I ever played was Super Mario on my sister's Super Nintendo.
    But I didn't play it all that much; my sisters were better than me at it anyway. My first game console was the original GameBoy.
    You know, the one that's the size of a brick and could survive a drop from a 10-story building? That one. It was great! I played
    Pokemon Red and got my Kadabra to level 100 before I knew it could evolve to Alakazam through a trade with a friend.

    Throughout my life, I owned other consoles like the Nintendo 64, GameCube, PlayStation 2, Xbox 360, PlayStation 3, 4, and 5.
    I loved the Zelda games, Ocarina of Time was my favorite out of the series. I'm also a Pokemon fan; Gotta Catch 'em All, am I right?
    I've played nearly all of them. However, most of my gaming career was on the PC when my parents bought my first computer.
    I was really addicted to MMOs like Runescape and World of Warcraft. I must admit, I still play Runescape (Old School version) to this day,
    even though it's a 20-year-old computer game. Other than Runescape, I love Rocket League. It's so fun, challenging, and technical,
    and did I mention it's really fun? I'll leave a shameless plug in here and drop my Old School Runescape username for anyone who plays
    to check out my stats on the high scores. I'm proud of my account! Heck, add me while you're at it! OSRS username: Imago Rei

  2. Drawing/Painting

    3. If I wasn't playing video games, I was drawing. I loved drawing my favorite anime and video game characters growing up.
    It was a way to express myself and relieve the stress of school and some gaming frustrations. At some point, I fell off from this hobby;
    in my 20s, most of my time was spent on work, gaming, and my relationship. It wasn't until I met my co-worker, Jonathan, from Road America,
    that my love for Art reignited. He was talented and produced fantastic work. He inspired me, and a fire sparked inside me to pick up the pencil again.
    Albeit I was rusty and nothing I produced ever came close to his work, I kept going. I practiced drawing cars at first, then eventually, people,
    portraits, and animals. This passion for Art is what eventually led me here today.

  3. Playing Music

    4. My mother forced me to play the Piano when I was in the 6th grade. I hated it.
    She hired a tutor, and for a whole year, I was forced to learn and play the instrument every weekend, which was awful. But you know what? I'm glad I did.
    I appreciate what my mother did for me, I just didn't know it then, but after I quit the Piano after a year of lessons, I returned to it and fell in love.
    I played the Piano for years, and I was getting good at it. Then in high school, I picked up the guitar and learned to play a variety of styles, like Classical
    on the acoustic and Metal on the electric. Unfortunately, I don't play the Piano anymore. I just don't have one! But I still pick up the guitar here and there
    and play my favorite song, Endlessly, from my favorite band, Muse.
